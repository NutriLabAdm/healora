## Description

Alternate Day Fasting (ADF) alternates between a "fast day" (0–25% of energy needs, ~500 kcal for women, ~600 kcal for men) and a "feed day" (ad libitum). Modified ADF allows ~500 kcal on fast days.

## Protocol Details

- **Regimen**: 24h fast (or ~500 kcal) on alternate days; eat ad libitum on feed days
- **Duration**: RCTs range 4–24 weeks; some studies up to 12 months
- **Adherence**: High (>80%) in short-term (<12 weeks); decreases with longer duration
- **Modified version**: 500–600 kcal on fast days (better adherence)

## Evidence

| Outcome | Effect | Certainty | Source |
|---------|--------|-----------|--------|
| Body weight loss | −3.4 to −5.1 kg vs ad-libitum | High (GRADE) | S001, S002 |
| Superiority vs CER | −1.29 kg additional loss | Moderate | S001 |
| Superiority vs TRE | −1.69 kg additional loss | Moderate | S001 |
| Superiority vs 5:2 | −1.05 kg additional loss | Moderate | S001 |
| BMI reduction | −1.5 to −2.0 kg/m² | High | S002 |
| Triglycerides | Significant reduction vs TRE/WDF | Moderate | S001 |
| Non-HDL cholesterol | Significant reduction | Moderate | S001 |

## Pros

- Highest weight loss among all IF forms per network meta-analyses
- Ranked #1 for metabolic health improvement in SUCRA analysis (S006)
- No daily calorie counting on feed days

## Cons

- Hardest to adhere to long-term
- Socially disruptive (every other day restriction)
- Hunger, dizziness, irritability on fast days
- Risk of overcompensation on feed days
- Not suitable for physically demanding jobs on fast days

## Who It's For

- Adults with obesity requiring structured intermittent restriction
- Individuals who prefer "all or nothing" approach
- Not recommended for: athletes in season, type 1 diabetes, eating disorder history, pregnancy

## Personalization Data Needed

- **Age, sex, BMI, body composition (DEXA/BIA)** — baseline
- **Caloric needs (TDEE)** — to set fast-day calorie cap (typically 20-25% of TDEE)
- **Work schedule & physical job demands** — ADF may be unsafe on heavy labor days
- **Psychological readiness** — screen for eating disorder risk (BED, orthorexia)
- **Previous IF experience** — naive vs experienced fasters adapt differently
- **Gastrointestinal health** — GERD, IBS may worsen on fast days
- **Social support & household environment** — crucial for adherence
- **Exercise schedule** — place workouts on feed days for performance

## Cost & Availability

- Free. Requires high motivation and social support.
- Apps: Zero, BodyFast (tracking fast/feed days)

## References

- S001: BMJ 2025 — Network meta-analysis, 99 RCTs
- S002: PubMed 2026 — 167 RCTs, 11998 participants
- S004: PubMed 2022 — 24 RCTs, 1768 participants. ADF ranked #1
