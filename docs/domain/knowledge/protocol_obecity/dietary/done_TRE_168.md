## Description

16:8 time-restricted eating involves a daily 16-hour fasting period followed by an 8-hour eating window. No calorie counting required during the eating window, though caloric intake is often spontaneously reduced by 200-400 kcal/day.

## Protocol Details

- **Regimen**: Fast 16h/day (e.g., 20:00–12:00), eat ad libitum during 8h window (e.g., 12:00–20:00)
- **Duration**: Ongoing lifestyle intervention; RCTs range 4–52 weeks
- **Adherence**: >80% in trials <12 weeks; drops to ~60-70% beyond 6 months
- **Variations**: 14:10 (milder), 18:6 (stricter), 20:4 (Warrior)

## Evidence

| Outcome | Effect | Certainty | Source |
|---------|--------|-----------|--------|
| Body weight loss | −2.0 to −3.5 kg vs ad-libitum | High (GRADE) | S001, S002 |
| BMI reduction | −1.0 to −1.4 kg/m² | Moderate | S007 |
| Waist circumference | −2.5 to −4.0 cm | Moderate | S007 |
| LDL cholesterol | −5.4 mg/dL | Moderate | S007 |
| Diastolic BP | −3.3 mmHg | Moderate | S007 |
| Fat mass loss | −0.7 kg | Low | S003 |

## Pros

- Simple: no calorie counting or food restrictions
- Easier adherence than continuous calorie restriction for many
- Compatible with social eating patterns (skip breakfast, eat lunch/dinner)

## Cons

- May cause hunger/irritability during adaptation (first 1-2 weeks)
- Headaches, dizziness reported (less than CER)
- Social challenges (breakfast meetings, late dinners)
- Long-term adherence declines significantly

## Who It's For

- Adults with overweight/obesity (BMI >25)
- Healthy adults seeking metabolic health improvements
- Not recommended for: underweight, pregnancy, eating disorder history, type 1 diabetes (without medical supervision)

## Personalization Data Needed

- **Age, sex, BMI, waist circumference** — baseline anthropometrics
- **Eating window preference** — earlier (10:00–18:00) vs later (12:00–20:00) based on chronotype
- **Work schedule & social commitments** — to pick compatible eating window
- **Hunger tolerance & previous dieting history** — predict adherence
- **Blood glucose (fasting), HbA1c** — if metabolic disease present
- **Medications** — especially insulin, sulfonylureas (risk of hypoglycemia during fast)
- **Chronotype (morning vs evening)** — determines optimal eating window
- **Physical activity timing** — workout placement relative to eating window

## Cost & Availability

- Free. No special foods or products needed.
- App support: Zero, FastHabit, MyFitnessPal (optional)

## References
