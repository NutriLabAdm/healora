## Description

Advanced ketogenic protocols for athletes and active individuals. **TKD**: consume 15-50g fast-acting carbs immediately before/after workouts. **CKD**: 5-6 days standard keto + 1-2 days high-carb "refeed" (designed by Lyle McDonald, "The Ketogenic Diet" 1998).

## Protocol Details

### TKD
- **Pre-workout**: 15-30g fast-acting carbs (glucose/dextrose) 15-30 min before training
- **Post-workout**: 15-30g carbs within 30 min post-training
- **Remaining time**: Standard keto (<30g carbs/day)

### CKD
- **5-6 days**: Standard keto (<30g carbs, high fat)
- **1-2 days**: High carb refeed (150-300g carbs, low fat, maintenance calories)
- **Weekly cycle**: Repeats weekly
- **Designed by**: Lyle McDonald, "The Ketogenic Diet" (1998) / bodybuilding community

## Evidence

- Very limited RCT evidence (S019 briefly covers, no specific TKD/CKD trials)
- Anecdotal evidence from bodybuilding and athletic community
- K-LCHF (ketogenic low-carb high-fat) reduced muscle mass by 1.27 kg in healthy participants (S019)
- TKD/CKD theoretically addresses exercise performance but lacks validation
- Complete evidence: insufficient for clinical recommendation

## Pros

- May support exercise performance on keto (TKD)
- Allows social eating on refeed days (CKD)
- May reduce muscle loss for athletes
- Psychological break from strict keto

## Cons

- Very complex to execute correctly
- Risk of never reaching or maintaining ketosis (CKD refeed days)
- High risk of bouncing between ketosis and carb-burning
- No robust evidence for superiority over standard keto or moderate low-carb
- Can trigger binge behavior on refeed days
- Requires meticulous tracking

## Personalization Data Needed

- **Age, sex, body composition (DEXA)** — baseline
- **Training volume & type** — determine if carb timing is needed
- **Training intensity** — HIIT/crossfit may benefit more than LISS
- **Ketone monitoring capability** — blood BHB to confirm ketosis
- **Previous keto experience** — only for keto-adapted individuals
- **Exercise performance tracking** — power output, endurance markers
- **Recovery metrics** — sleep, HRV, perceived recovery
- **Psychological readiness** — ability to handle complex protocols

## Who It's For

- Keto-adapted athletes (≥4 weeks deep keto first)
- Bodybuilders/pre-contest physique athletes
- High-volume endurance/strength athletes struggling on standard keto
- Not recommended for general population seeking weight loss
- Insufficient evidence for clinical recommendation

## Cost & Availability

- Free (if already on keto) — just adds carb timing
- Requires precision tracking (Carb Manager, Cronometer)
- Blood ketone meter recommended ($30-50 + $2-3/test strip)
