## Description

A dietary pattern based on traditional eating habits of Greece, Italy, and Spain. High in olive oil, vegetables, fruits, legumes, whole grains, nuts, fish; moderate in poultry, eggs, dairy; low in red meat and sweets. Often combined with moderate red wine consumption.

## Protocol Details

- **Regimen**: Ad libitum (non-calorie-restricted) or hypocaloric version for weight loss
- **Key components**: EVOO ≥4 tbsp/day, vegetables ≥3 servings/day, fish ≥2x/week, nuts ≥3x/week, red meat <1x/week
- **Duration**: RCTs range 6 months to 5+ years; designed as lifelong eating pattern
- **Adherence**: Moderate (60-70% at 1 year); higher than low-fat diets in RCTs
- **Caloric version**: Hypocaloric MED with 500-750 kcal deficit for weight loss

## Evidence

| Outcome | Effect | Certainty | Source |
|---------|--------|-----------|--------|
| Body weight loss (hypocaloric) | −2.4 to −4.0 kg vs control | Moderate | S008, S012 |
| BMI reduction | −0.61 to −0.71 kg/m² | Moderate | S010, S013 |
| Waist circumference | −1.7 to −2.5 cm | Moderate | S010 |
| HbA1c reduction (T2DM) | −0.39% | High | S011 |
| CV mortality risk reduction | −35% (MI/stroke/CV death) | High | S010 |
| Triglycerides | −7.9 mg/dL | Low | S010 |
| Fatty liver index | −12.3 | Moderate | S010 |

## Pros

- Most extensively studied dietary pattern globally
- Reduces all-cause and CV mortality (PREDIMED trial)
- #1 for glycemic control in T2DM (SUCRA 88%, S011)
- No calorie counting in non-restricted version
- Rich in antioxidants, anti-inflammatory
- Sustainable as lifelong eating pattern
- Enjoyable: allows wine, olive oil, flavor

## Cons

- Modest weight loss (−2 to −4 kg) compared to low-carb/keto (−6 to −10 kg, S009)
- Requires significant cooking and meal prep
- Can be expensive (olive oil, fresh fish, nuts)
- Not suitable for rapid weight loss
- Long-term adherence requires cultural/environmental support
- Evidence quality mostly low-to-moderate per GRADE (S010)

## Who It's For

- Cardiovascular disease prevention / metabolic syndrome
- Type 2 diabetes (glycemic control priority)
- Long-term sustainable weight management (not rapid loss)
- Individuals who prefer a lifestyle pattern over a restrictive "diet"
- Contraindicated: no specific contraindications; caution with wine in addiction history

## Personalization Data Needed

- **Age, sex, BMI, waist circumference** — baseline
- **Lipid panel (TC, LDL, HDL, TG)** — assess CV risk
- **Fasting glucose, HbA1c** — metabolic health baseline
- **Blood pressure** — MED + exercise improves BP
- **Cultural food preferences** — MED can be adapted to local cuisines
- **Cooking ability & time** — MED requires fresh food preparation
- **Budget** — olive oil, fish, nuts are costlier
- **Alcohol preference** — wine component optional, contraindicated in some
- **Allergies/intolerances** — fish, nuts, dairy
- **Family support & household eating patterns** — crucial for adherence

## Cost & Availability

- Moderate cost: $80–120/week per person (higher than standard Western diet)
- Widely adaptable across cuisines
- Book support: "The Mediterranean Diet" by Dr. Walter Willett, PREDIMED cookbooks
