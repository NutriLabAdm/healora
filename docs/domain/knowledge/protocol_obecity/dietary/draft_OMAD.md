## Description

OMAD (23:1) restricts all daily calories to a single 1-hour eating window, resulting in a 23-hour daily fast. An extreme form of time-restricted eating.

## Protocol Details

- **Regimen**: One meal within 1–2 hour window daily (e.g., 18:00–19:00)
- **Duration**: Few RCTs; most evidence from case series and short-term trials (2–12 weeks)
- **Adherence**: Low (<50% in longer trials); very difficult for most people
- **Caloric intake**: Often spontaneously drops to 1200–1500 kcal/day

## Evidence

- Limited high-quality RCTs
- Short-term weight loss (2–6 kg over 4–12 weeks) — but likely driven by extreme caloric deficit
- Risk of muscle loss: very high (no protein distribution across the day)
- Complete evidence: insufficient

## Pros

- Extremely simple — no decisions about when/what to eat
- Very large caloric deficit naturally
- Liberates significant time

## Cons

- Very high hunger, irritability, brain fog
- Poor nutrient absorption (single meal may not meet micronutrient needs)
- High muscle catabolism risk
- Binge risk during the one meal
- Unsustainable for most people
- Socially isolating

## Who It's For

- Highly disciplined individuals seeking rapid weight loss
- Not recommended for anyone with metabolic disease, diabetes, muscle wasting concerns, or without medical supervision

## Personalization Data Needed

- **Full medical workup** — mandatory before starting extreme IF
- **Body composition (DEXA)** — track muscle loss closely
- **Metabolic rate (RMR)** — prevent excessive caloric deficit
- **Micronutrient status (iron, B12, vitamin D, zinc)** — single meal may not meet needs
- **Psychological assessment** — high risk for disordered eating
- **Work & physical demands** — not compatible with heavy labor or intense training
- **Hormonal status** — women may experience menstrual cycle disruption
- **Blood glucose monitoring** — risk of hypoglycemia
- **Reason for choosing OMAD** — distinguish preference vs pathology

## Cost & Availability

- Free. Requires significant mental fortitude.
