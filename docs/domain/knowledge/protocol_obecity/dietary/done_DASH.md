## Description

The DASH diet emphasizes fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and low-fat dairy while limiting sodium, saturated fat, and added sugars. Originally designed to lower blood pressure, it also promotes weight loss and metabolic health.

## Protocol Details

- **Regimen**: 2 versions — standard DASH (2300 mg Na/day) and lower-sodium DASH (1500 mg Na/day)
- **Daily servings**: Grains 6-8, vegetables 4-5, fruits 4-5, dairy 2-3, lean meat 2 or fewer, nuts/seeds 4-5/week, fats/oils 2-3, sweets <5/week
- **Duration**: RCTs 4–52 weeks; designed as lifelong pattern
- **Adherence**: Moderate (55-70%); higher with counseling support
- **Caloric version**: Reduced-calorie DASH (1200–1600 kcal/day) for weight loss

## Pros

- #1 for blood pressure control among all diets (SUCRA 89%, S014)
- Reduces LDL and triglycerides
- Well-studied: 50+ RCTs across diverse populations
- Nutrient-dense: high in fiber, potassium, calcium, magnesium
- Flexible: allows all food groups in moderation
- Safe for long-term use
- Reduces diabetes risk by 18% (RR 0.82, 557K participants)

## Cons

- Modest weight loss only (−1.5 kg average)
- High dairy requirement (2-3 servings/day) — issue for lactose intolerance
- Requires significant meal planning
- Low sodium requires label reading and cooking from scratch
- Weight loss effects inferior to low-carb and keto diets (S014)
- Not designed specifically for weight loss (primary purpose is BP control)

## Who It's For

- Hypertension / prehypertension (primary indication)
- Metabolic syndrome
- Cardiovascular disease prevention
- Adults needing modest weight loss with BP control
- Contraindicated: advanced chronic kidney disease (high potassium), lactose intolerance (modify dairy)

## Personalization Data Needed

- **Age, sex, BMI, waist circumference** — baseline
- **Blood pressure (office + ambulatory)** — primary target
- **Serum potassium, creatinine, eGFR** — risk of hyperkalemia in CKD
- **Lipid panel, fasting glucose** — metabolic baseline
- **Lactose tolerance** — DASH requires 2-3 dairy servings/day
- **Cooking habits & sodium awareness** — low-sodium version requires label reading
- **Cultural food patterns** — DASH can be adapted (Asian, Latin, etc.)
- **Budget** — fresh produce requirements increase cost
- **Medication review** — diuretics + low-sodium DASH may cause electrolyte shifts
- **Food allergies** — nuts, dairy, gluten

## Cost & Availability

- Moderate cost: $70–100/week per person
- Widely supported by AHA, NIH, USDA guidelines
- Meal plans freely available at https://www.nhlbi.nih.gov/education/dash-eating-plan
- App support: DASH Diet app, MyFitnessPal
