## Description

Commercial weight loss programs provide structured, protocol-driven interventions combining dietary guidance, behavioral support, self-monitoring, and accountability. Market leaders: WW (Weight Watchers), Noom, Nutrisystem, Jenny Craig, Optifast, Medifast, SlimFast. Evidence supports modest weight loss superior to DIY/control, with WW and Jenny Craig having strongest long-term data.

## Protocol Details

### WW (Weight Watchers)
- **Method**: Points-based food tracking system (ZeroPoint foods + personalized Points budget)
- **Support**: Weekly workshops (in-person or virtual) + app + coach
- **12-mo weight loss**: −2.6% to −4.4% above control/DIY (S056, S058)
- **Evidence**: Multiple RCTs, Grade A (Canadian guidelines)

### Noom
- **Method**: Psychology-based (CBT principles), color-coded food logging (green/yellow/red)
- **Support**: App-based + coach + group support
- **Weight loss**: −0.8 to −3.8 kg at 4-6 mo (S057, app-based studies)
- **Evidence**: Moderate; fewer long-term RCTs than WW

### Nutrisystem
- **Method**: Prepackaged meals (~1200-1500 kcal/day) + snacks
- **3-mo weight loss**: −3.8% above control (S056)
- **Evidence**: Level 1b, Grade B (Canadian guidelines)

### Jenny Craig
- **Method**: Prepackaged meals + weekly individual counseling (in-person or phone)
- **12-mo weight loss**: −4.9% above control/counseling (S056)
- **T2DM benefit**: −0.4 to −0.8% HbA1c reduction
- **Evidence**: Level 1b, Grade B

### Very-Low-Calorie Programs (Optifast, Medifast)
- **Method**: Meal replacements 800-1200 kcal/day + medical supervision
- **Weight loss**: −4.0% short-term above counseling; attenuates >6 mo (S056)

## Evidence

| Program | 3-6 mo vs Control | 12 mo vs Control | Certainty | Source |
|---------|-------------------|-------------------|-----------|--------|

## Pros

- Structured, easy-to-follow protocols
- Built-in accountability (weekly weigh-ins, group support, coaching)
- Social support (groups, community)
- Personalized (adapted to preferences, goals)
- Noom: psychology-based approach addresses emotional eating
- WW: flexible points system, no banned foods
- Meal replacement programs (Nutrisystem, Jenny Craig): remove food decisions
- Wide availability (in-person + digital)
- Insurance discounts available (WW covered by some)

## Cons

- Modest weight loss, comparable to self-directed diet, less than GLP-1 or surgery
- Cost: $15-80/week (WW $23/mo, Noom $60-200/yr, Nutrisystem $250-350/mo)
- High attrition beyond 6 months
- Meal replacement programs: low sustainability, weight regain on transition
- Noom: mixed evidence, no large long-term RCTs
- Very-low-calorie programs require medical supervision
- Not suitable for severe obesity without adjunctive treatment
- Commercial conflicts of interest in some published trials

## Personalization Data Needed

- **Budget** — programs range $15-350/month
- **Schedule** — weekly meetings vs app-only flexibility
- **Food preferences** — prepackaged meals vs flexible points vs cooking
- **Tech comfort** — Noom is app-only; WW hybrid; Jenny Craig more traditional
- **Weight loss goal** — align with expected outcomes
- **Comorbidities** — T2DM (Jenny Craig strongest HbA1c data)
- **Support preference** — group, individual coaching, or self-guided
- **Previous dieting history** — which programs tried before
- **T2DM status** — some programs offer diabetes-specific versions
- **Pregnancy/breastfeeding** — very-low-calorie contraindicated

## Who It's For

- Overweight/obesity (BMI 25-35) seeking structured support
- First-line structured lifestyle intervention
- Individuals who prefer group/coach accountability
- Maintenance after initial loss (WW has strongest long-term data)
- T2DM with obesity (Jenny Craig, WW have diabetes-specific programs)
- Contraindicated: very-low-calorie programs in pregnancy, eating disorders, uncontrolled psychiatric illness; individual program restrictions

## Cost & Availability

- WW: $23/month (digital) to $70/month (workshops + digital); free 7-day trial
- Noom: $60-200/year (varies by plan/length); 14-day trial
- Nutrisystem: $250-350/month (food); $100-200/month (select)
- Jenny Craig: $200-350/month (food); $50-100/month (counseling)
- Optifast: $200-400/month (medical supervision extra)
- Some insurance/employer wellness programs subsidize or cover (WW covered by ~50% US insurers)
- Medicare covers WW for qualifying beneficiaries (diabetes prevention)
