## Description

Obesity management varies substantially across special populations due to different pathophysiology, treatment goals, safety considerations, and evidence bases. Four key populations require tailored approaches: (1) children and adolescents (pediatric obesity), (2) older adults ≥65 years (geriatric/sarcopenic obesity), (3) women with polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS), and (4) individuals with type 2 diabetes (diabetic obesity). This protocol synthesizes population-specific evidence for lifestyle, pharmacotherapy, and surgical interventions.

## Protocol Details

### 1. Pediatric Obesity (Children & Adolescents)

**Epidemiology**: BMI ≥95th percentile affects ~20% of US youth; prevalence rising globally.

**Guidelines**: AAP 2023 Clinical Practice Guideline — recommend offering AOM to youth ≥12 years with obesity, and bariatric surgery referral for BMI ≥35 with comorbidity or ≥40 (S075).

**First-line**: Intensive Health Behavior and Lifestyle Treatment (IHBLT) — ≥26 contact hours over 3-12 months, family-based, face-to-face (S075). Effect: BMI −0.7 (28 RCTs, n=4494, S076).

**Pharmacotherapy** (35 RCTs, n=4331, S078):
- Semaglutide: best — BMI −5.88 kg/m² (1 RCT, n=201, S078)
- Phentermine/Topiramate: comparable efficacy, BMI −4.5 to −6.0 (S077)
- Liraglutide: BMI −1.71 (moderate effect, S078)
- Metformin: BMI −0.8 to −1.2, improves insulin resistance
- Orlistat: BMI −0.5 to −1.0, GI side effects
- GI AEs common with GLP-1s and orlistat; serious AEs rare (<1%) (S078)
- Weight regain upon discontinuation immediate (S076)

**Surgery**: RYGB/SG for severe adolescent obesity; limited data but similar efficacy to adults. AAP recommends BMI ≥35 with comorbidity or BMI ≥40 (S075).

### 2. Geriatric Obesity (≥65 years, Sarcopenic Obesity)

**Epidemiology**: Affects up to 1/3 of older adults globally; prevalence increasing with population aging (S079).

**Key concept**: Sarcopenic obesity — concurrent decline in muscle mass/function + increased adiposity. Focus on preserving functional capacity and QoL, not weight loss alone (S079, S080).

**EASO 2025 Position Statement** (S079):
- BMI alone insufficient; assess body composition (fat distribution + muscle mass), psychological status, functional performance
- Multimodal approach: moderate energy restriction (250-500 kcal deficit) + adequate protein (1.2-1.5 g/kg/day) + structured multicomponent exercise (RT + AT) + behavioral support
- Consider GLP-1 RA (semaglutide, liraglutide) where appropriate; caution with frailty/sarcopenia
- Bariatric surgery possible in selected older adults

**Exercise by modality** (S082, 8 RCTs):
- Resistance training (RT): best for grip strength (+3.85 kg), walking speed (+0.23 m/s), knee extension (+4.85), %BF (−2.96%) — no weight change
- Aerobic training (AT): best for weight reduction (−6.07 kg) — no strength benefit
- Mixed training (MT): improves both weight (−4.40 kg) and %BF (−2.42%), walking speed (+0.15)

**Pharmacotherapy caution** (S080):
- GLP-1 RA most promising for weight + body composition in older adults, but no official guidelines exist
- Risk: lean mass reduction in frail patients with sarcopenic obesity
- Semaglutide OASIS 1 trial included older adults; safety profile acceptable
- No specific drugs approved for sarcopenic obesity (S081)

**Nutritional priorities**: High protein (1.2-1.5 g/kg), vitamin D 800-1000 IU/day, calcium. Avoid rapid weight loss >0.5-1 kg/week.

### 3. PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome)

**Epidemiology**: 8-13% of reproductive-age women; 50-80% have overweight/obesity. Bidirectional relationship: obesity worsens PCOS, PCOS promotes weight gain.

**First-line**: Lifestyle modification (diet + exercise) — cornerstone (S083). HIIT improves IR; TRF improves hyperandrogenism; ketogenic diet improves both reproductive and metabolic outcomes.

**Pharmacotherapy** (S083, S084):
- GLP-1 RA + metformin: best weight loss — −6.65 kg (10 RCTs, n=554, S084)
- GLP-1 RA alone: −5.85 kg
- SGLT2i + metformin: −4.74 kg
- Metformin alone: −3.93 kg, BMI −1.64, improved insulin, fasting glucose, menstrual regularity
- Phentermine/Topiramate: −11.99 kg (best among all, but limited data in PCOS, S084)
- Liraglutide + metformin: better than metformin alone for BMI, glucose, lipids, hormones, menstrual cycles (12 RCTs, n=1096, S083)
- Metformin alone consistent: improved fasting insulin, HOMA-IR, serum testosterone, moderate BMI/WC reduction (S084)

**Hormonal/metabolic improvements** from weight loss (29 RCTs, n=1529, S083):
- HOMA-IR: −0.45; FAI: −2.03; menstrual frequency: +2.64 cycles/year
- No significant improvement in hirsutism or QoL from weight loss alone

**Surgery**: Bariatric surgery more effective than pharmacotherapy for PCOS-related metabolic outcomes; limited head-to-head data vs GLP-1 RA (S083).

**Key notes**: Longer treatment duration (≥16 weeks) and combination therapy (GLP-1 + metformin) associated with greater improvements. Individualize based on PCOS phenotype (obese vs lean, insulin-resistant vs not).

### 4. Diabetic Obesity (T2DM + Obesity)

**Epidemiology**: ~90% of T2DM patients have overweight/obesity. Weight loss is central to T2DM management and remission.

**Tiered approach**:
- Lifestyle + weight management → metformin → GLP-1 RA / SGLT2i → combination → insulin (weight-neutral or sparing) → bariatric surgery

**GLP-1 RAs in T2DM** (64 RCTs, n=25,572, S085):
- Tirzepatide: best HbA1c reduction (−2.3%), weight −9.1 kg — most suitable for T2DM + obesity
- Semaglutide: HbA1c −1.5%, weight −2.8 kg; hypoglycemia risk increased vs placebo
- Liraglutide: HbA1c −1.2%, weight −1.2 kg; lowest hypoglycemia risk — optimal for normal-weight T2DM
- All GLP-1 RAs: GI AEs common; no significant BMI/SBP/DBP/TC/HDL/LDL changes vs placebo

**Lifestyle + GLP-1 RA combination** (33 RCTs, n=12,028, S086):
- Weight loss: −7.13 kg; WC −5.74 cm; SBP −3.99 mmHg; FBG −6.51 mg/dL
- Fat mass: −2.93 kg; lean mass: −1.29 kg (concern in frail)
- Semaglutide and tirzepatide weekly dosing showed most pronounced effects
- GRADE: high certainty for HDL, LDL, body fat%; moderate-low for weight, BP, glycemia

**Surgery vs GLP-1 RA in T2DM** (S087, S088):
- MBS: 28.3% TWL vs GLP-1 RA: 10.3% TWL at 2 years (p<0.001, S088)
- MACE reduction: HR 0.65 for MBS vs GLP-1 RA (S087)
- All-cause mortality: HR 0.68 for MBS vs GLP-1 RA (S087)
- MACE risk reduction: ~52% in favor of MBS (S089)
- Nephropathy: HR 0.53; Retinopathy: HR 0.46
- Cost: MBS break-even at ~15 months vs GLP-1 RA ($11,689 lower cost at 2yr, S088)
- Surgery superior for durable weight loss and cardiovascular protection but invasive

**T2DM remission** (Nature Medicine NMA, S085):
- Tirzepatide and semaglutide: normoglycemia restoration, T2DM remission, reduced HF hospitalization
- Tirzepatide: effective for OSA and MASH remission

## Evidence Summary

| Population | Best Lifestyle | Best Pharmacotherapy | Surgery Role | Key Source |
|------------|---------------|---------------------|-------------|------------|

## Pros

- AAP 2023 guidelines and FDA approvals now support AOM use in adolescents ≥12
- Semaglutide highly effective in pediatric obesity (BMI −5.88, largest effect among all pediatric AOMs)
- Geriatric obesity management shifts focus from weight loss alone to functional preservation + muscle mass → more clinically meaningful for older adults
- EASO 2025 provides clear framework for older adult obesity management (multimodal, individualized)
- RT improves grip strength, walking speed, and knee extension in sarcopenic obesity — meaningful for frailty prevention
- PCOS: GLP-1 + metformin combination addresses both metabolic and reproductive dysfunction
- T2DM: tirzepatide provides dual benefit (HbA1c −2.3% + weight −9.1 kg) in single agent
- Lifestyle + GLP-1 combination in T2DM: robust evidence (33 RCTs, 12,028 participants)
- MBS superior to GLP-1 RA for MACE, mortality, nephropathy, retinopathy in T2DM
- MBS more cost-effective than GLP-1 RAs beyond 15 months

## Cons

- Pediatric pharmacotherapy: immediate weight regain upon discontinuation; no data beyond 17 months
- Semaglutide in adolescents limited to 1 RCT (n=201); generalizability concerns
- Geriatric obesity: no official pharmacotherapy guidelines for ≥65; most drug trials excluded older adults
- GLP-1 RAs reduce lean mass (−1.29 kg in T2DM combination), which may worsen sarcopenia
- PCOS: metformin + GLP-1 GI side effects common; long-term pregnancy safety data lacking
- T2DM: semaglutide increases hypoglycemia risk vs placebo; liraglutide has lower but still present risk
- Bariatric surgery in T2DM carries perioperative risks, lifelong nutritional monitoring, and is not universally accessible
- Geriatric bariatric surgery: limited data in ≥70; higher complication risk
- PCOS weight loss interventions do not improve hirsutism or QoL based on current evidence
- Pediatric AOM: total of 4 drug classes approved; no head-to-head trials with adequate long-term follow-up

## Personalization Data Needed

- **Age** — pediatric vs adult vs geriatric protocols differ fundamentally
- **BMI percentile (children)** — ≥95th vs ≥120% of 95th for treatment thresholds
- **Frailty status / functional capacity (geriatric)** — grip strength, gait speed, chair stand test
- **Body composition** — muscle mass (DEXA/BIA) critical for geriatric and PCOS populations
- **PCOS phenotype** — A (hyperandrogenism + anovulation) vs B vs C vs D; affects treatment choice
- **T2DM duration and control** — HbA1c, medications, complications; affects surgery timing
- **Cardiovascular risk** — MACE history critical for T2DM treatment selection (GLP-1 vs SGLT2i vs surgery)
- **Fertility goals (PCOS)** — metformin preferred pre-conception; GLP-1 contraindicated in pregnancy
- **Comorbidities** — renal function (SGLT2i), liver (tirzepatide for MASH), OSA (tirzepatide), HF
- **Pediatric: pubertal stage** — Tanner staging affects AOM safety assessment
- **Pediatric: family support** — IHBLT requires family engagement for success
- **Geriatric: polypharmacy** — drug interactions with GLP-1 RAs
- **Geriatric: cognitive status** — ability to adhere to complex multimodal regimens
- **Socioeconomic** — access to surgery, GLP-1 RA cost ($900-1400/month without insurance)

## Who It's For

- **Pediatric**: Youth aged 2-18 with BMI ≥95th percentile; IHBLT for all, AOM for ≥12, surgery for ≥35+comorbidity or ≥40
- **Geriatric**: Adults ≥65 with obesity (BMI ≥30) or sarcopenic obesity; focus on functional preservation
- **PCOS**: Reproductive-age women with PCOS + overweight/obesity; GLP-1 + metformin for metabolic + reproductive benefit
- **T2DM**: Adults with T2DM + obesity; tirzepatide for glycemic + weight control; MBS for MACE reduction
- **NOT for**: Frail older adults with sarcopenia (aggressive energy restriction or GLP-1 without RT); children <12 without pediatric obesity specialist; PCOS with normal weight (metformin only if insulin-resistant); T2DM without obesity (GLP-1 RA for glycemic control but liraglutide preferred over tirzepatide to minimize hypoglycemia risk)

## Cost & Availability

- **Pediatric IHBLT**: $50-200/session, 26+ hours → $1300-5200 total; insurance coverage varies
- **Pediatric AOM**: semaglutide ~$900-1400/month; liraglutide ~$1200-1600/month; insurance may cover if AAP guideline followed
- **Geriatric multimodal**: RT gym membership $30-80/month + dietician $50-150/session + GLP-1 $900-1400/month; Medicare Part D covers GLP-1 for T2DM but not obesity-only
- **PCOS metformin**: $4-30/month (generic); GLP-1 + metformin combination $900-1600/month
- **T2DM tirzepatide**: ~$1000-1300/month; insurance typically covers with T2DM diagnosis
- **Bariatric surgery**: $15,000-30,000 total; insurance covers if medical necessity criteria met; break-even vs GLP-1 at ~15 months (S088)
- Global: pediatric AOM available in US/EU but limited in LMICs; surgery available in most urban centers worldwide; metformin universally available and inexpensive
