## Description

Older-generation weight loss pharmacotherapies and oral agents: orlistat (lipase inhibitor), metformin (AMPK activator, weight-neutral to modest), phentermine/topiramate (sympathomimetic + anticonvulsant), naltrexone/bupropion (opioid antagonist + NDRI). These remain important options, especially when GLP-1 access is limited.

## Protocol Details

### Orlistat (Xenical/Alli)
- **Dose**: 120 mg TID with meals (OTC: 60 mg TID)
- **Mechanism**: Inhibits pancreatic/gastric lipase → reduces fat absorption by ~30%
- **Duration**: Up to 4 years in XENDOS trial
- **Adherence**: 40-60% at 1 year (GI side effects)

### Metformin (off-label for weight)
- **Dose**: 500-2000 mg/day (start 500 mg, titrate weekly)
- **Mechanism**: AMPK activation, reduces hepatic gluconeogenesis, improves insulin sensitivity
- **Weight effect**: −2 to −4 kg average (not FDA-approved for weight; used in prediabetes/PCOS)
- **Duration**: Long-term safe; DPP trial 2.8 year follow-up

### Phentermine/Topiramate (Qsymia)
- **Dose**: Phentermine 7.5-15 mg + topiramate 46-92 mg once daily
- **Mechanism**: Phentermine = sympathomimetic appetite suppression; topiramate = GABA modulation + carbonic anhydrase inhibition
- **Adherence**: 50-65% at 1 year (cognitive side effects)

### Naltrexone/Bupropion (Contrave)
- **Dose**: Naltrexone 32 mg + bupropion 360 mg (2 tabs BID)
- **Mechanism**: Naltrexone blocks opioid reward; bupropion inhibits dopamine/norepinephrine reuptake
- **Adherence**: 40-50% at 1 year (nausea, insomnia)

## Pros

### Orlistat
- OTC availability (Alli 60 mg)
- No systemic absorption (acts in gut)
- CV benefit: reduces LDL, BP
- Longest safety data (20+ years)
- Low cost

### Metformin
- Very low cost ($10-20/month)
- Excellent safety profile
- Weight-neutral to modest loss
- PCOS indication
- Reduces T2DM progression (DPP trial −31%)

### Phentermine/Topiramate
- Highest efficacy among non-GLP-1 agents (−7-9 kg)
- Oral, once daily
- Available as generic (lower cost)
- Topiramate beneficial for binge eating

### Naltrexone/Bupropion
- Oral, twice daily
- Reduces food reward/craving
- Bupropion may improve mood
- No injection

## Cons

### Orlistat
- GI side effects: oily spotting, flatus, fecal urgency (common)
- Fat-soluble vitamin malabsorption (A, D, E, K)
- Modest weight loss only
- TID dosing with meals

### Metformin
- Not FDA-approved for weight loss (off-label)
- Modest effect (−2 to −4 kg)
- GI side effects (diarrhea, nausea)
- Lactic acidosis risk (rare, CKD contraindicated)

### Phentermine/Topiramate
- Cognitive side effects (word-finding difficulty, memory issues)
- Topiramate teratogenic (cleft palate)
- Phentermine increases HR + BP
- Insomnia, dry mouth, constipation
- Controlled substance (CIV)

### Naltrexone/Bupropion
- Nausea (common, especially at initiation)
- Bupropion seizure risk (contraindicated in seizure disorders, eating disorders)
- Insomnia, headache
- Black box: suicidal ideation (bupropion)
- BP increase

## Personalization Data Needed

- **BMI, comorbidities** — indication
- **Lipid profile** — orlistat reduces LDL
- **Blood pressure** — phentermine raises, orlistat lowers
- **eGFR, liver function** — metformin contraindicated if eGFR <30
- **Type 2 diabetes / prediabetes** — metformin first-line
- **PCOS** — metformin beneficial
- **Headache/migraine history** — topiramate may benefit
- **Seizure/eating disorder history** — bupropion contraindicated
- **Pregnancy risk** — X-ray results for women of childbearing age (topiramate teratogenic)
- **Cost/insurance coverage** — generics much cheaper
- **GI tolerance** — orlistat side effects, metformin GI

## Who It's For

- First-line oral options when GLP-1 unavailable/unaffordable
- Orlistat: OTC option, modest loss, CV benefit
- Metformin: prediabetes, PCOS, T2DM + weight management
- Phentermine/Topiramate: highest non-GLP-1 efficacy
- Naltrexone/Bupropion: food craving, mood component
- Contraindicated: orlistat= malabsorption; metformin= CKD stage 4+; phen/top= uncontrolled HTN, glaucoma; nalt/bup= seizure, bulimia, opioid use

## Cost & Availability

- Orlistat: $30-60/month (OTC); $120-200 (Rx Xenical)
- Metformin: $5-20/month (generic)
- Phentermine/Topiramate: $30-100/month (generic); $200-300 (Qsymia brand)
- Naltrexone/Bupropion: $80-200/month (generic); $300-400 (Contrave)
- Widely available, no refrigeration needed
- Most insurance covers all except Contrave (variable)
