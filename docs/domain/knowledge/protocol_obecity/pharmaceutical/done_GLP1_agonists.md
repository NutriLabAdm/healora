## Description

GLP-1 receptor agonists mimic endogenous glucagon-like peptide-1, increasing insulin secretion, delaying gastric emptying, and reducing appetite via central and peripheral mechanisms. Semaglutide (Wegovy 2.4 mg) and liraglutide (Saxenda 3.0 mg) are FDA-approved for chronic weight management.

## Protocol Details

- **Semaglutide**: 2.4 mg SC once weekly. Dose escalation: 0.25→0.5→1.0→1.7→2.4 mg over 16-20 weeks
- **Liraglutide**: 3.0 mg SC once daily. Dose escalation: 0.6→1.2→1.8→2.4→3.0 mg over 5 weeks
- **Duration**: Continuous (chronic therapy); 68-week pivotal trials; weight regain if discontinued
- **Adherence**: 60-80% at 1 year; GI side effects main reason for discontinuation
- **Oral semaglutide**: 50 mg once daily (Rybelsus) — newer option

## Evidence

| Outcome | Effect | Certainty | Source |
|---------|--------|-----------|--------|
| Semaglutide TBWL% (68 wk) | −13.9 to −15.8% vs placebo −2.4% | High | S035, S037 |
| Liraglutide TBWL% (26 wk) | −5.8% vs placebo | High | S037 |
| ≥5% weight loss (semaglutide) | 78.5% vs 26.5% placebo; OR 11.4 | High | S036 |
| ≥10% weight loss (semaglutide) | ~69% vs placebo | High | S037 |
| MACE reduction (semaglutide) | HR 0.80 (SELECT trial) | High | S035 |
| HbA1c reduction (T2DM) | −1.5 to −2.0% | High | S038 |

## Pros

- Most effective pharmacotherapy for weight loss (after tirzepatide)
- CV benefit: reduces major adverse CV events (SELECT trial)
- Improves glycemic control in T2DM
- Reduces progression to T2DM in prediabetes
- Once-weekly dosing (semaglutide) — convenient
- Addresses biological drivers of obesity (hunger hormones)

## Cons

- GI side effects: nausea 14-28%, vomiting 6-12%, diarrhea 8-20%
- Dose titration required (slow escalation)
- High cost ($800-1400/month without insurance)
- Weight regain on discontinuation (~70% regain within 1 year)
- Injectable (oral option limited bioavailability)
- Thyroid C-cell tumor risk (black box warning)
- Pancreatitis risk (rare)
- Requires refrigeration
- Not for pregnancy/breastfeeding

## Personalization Data Needed

- **BMI and weight history** — indication (BMI ≥30 or ≥27 with comorbidity)
- **Type 2 diabetes status** — HbA1c, fasting glucose (GLP-1 preferred if T2DM)
- **CV risk assessment** — prior MI, stroke (SELECT trial showed benefit)
- **eGFR** — no dose adjustment needed but monitor if severe impairment
- **Liver function** — MASH resolution benefit (tirzepatide)
- **History of pancreatitis** — contraindication (relative)
- **Family history of thyroid cancer (MTC)** — contraindication
- **Pregnancy/breastfeeding** — contraindicated
- **GI history** — severe gastroparesis, GERD (may worsen)
- **Insurance coverage** — prior authorization often required
- **Needle phobia** — may prefer oral semaglutide or alternatives
- **Cost tolerance** — out-of-pocket vs insurance coverage

## Who It's For

- Adults with BMI ≥30 or BMI ≥27 + weight-related comorbidity
- T2DM with overweight/obesity (metformin + GLP-1)
- High CV risk (semaglutide proven CV benefit)
- Individuals who failed lifestyle interventions
- Contraindicated: MTC family history, MEN-2, pancreatitis history, pregnancy, severe gastroparesis

## Cost & Availability

- High: $800-1,400/month (Wegovy $1,349, Saxenda $1,200)
- Insurance coverage improving (60-70% coverage for T2DM; 40% for obesity)
- Patient assistance programs available (Novo Nordisk)
- Available in most countries; lower cost in EU
- Self-pay: online clinics (Ro, Calibrate, Found) $100-400/month + medication cost
