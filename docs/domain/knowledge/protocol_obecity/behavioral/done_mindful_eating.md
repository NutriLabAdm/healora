## Description

Mindfulness-based interventions (MBIs) for weight management train non-judgmental awareness of hunger, fullness, cravings, and emotional eating triggers. Includes mindful eating exercises, body scan, meditation, and formal mindfulness practice. Typically 6-8 weekly group sessions.

## Protocol Details

- **Format**: Group sessions (7-8 weekly × 90 min) + daily home practice (15-30 min)
- **Core practices**: Raisin exercise, hunger/fullness awareness, craving surfing, body scan, loving-kindness
- **Duration**: 6-12 weeks initial; booster sessions optional
- **Adherence**: 60-75% session completion; lower than CBT but acceptable
- **Variants**: MB-EAT (Mindfulness-Based Eating Awareness Training), Mind-Eat program

## Evidence

| Outcome | Effect | Certainty | Source |
|--------|--------|-----------|--------|
| Weight loss (immediate) | Hedge's g = 0.39 (small-moderate) | Moderate | S032 |
| BMI reduction (immediate) | Hedge's g = 0.62 (moderate-large) | Moderate | S032 |
| Binge eating reduction | g = −0.35 (not significant, p=0.136) | Low | S031 |
| Emotional eating reduction | g = −0.27 (not significant, p=0.07) | Low | S031 |
| Uncontrolled eating reduction | Significant improvement | Moderate | S030 |
| Food craving intensity | g = 0.28 (significant reduction) | Low | S034 |
| Weight maintenance (6-12 mo) | Comparable to standard diet | Low | S032 |

## Pros

- Improves eating behaviors (uncontrolled eating, external eating)
- Reduces food craving intensity
- No restrictive rules — sustainable approach
- Improves psychological well-being
- Safe, no adverse effects
- Can be combined with any dietary protocol
- Effective for binge eating behaviors (not just weight)

## Cons

- Small-modest weight loss (g = 0.39-0.62 effect size)
- Mixed evidence for BMI improvement in longer term
- Requires daily practice commitment
- Less effective for weight loss than dietary restriction alone
- Higher attrition in meditation-only groups
- Inconsistent evidence for emotional/binge eating reduction
- Limited evidence in children (BMI not significant, S033)

## Personalization Data Needed

- **Eating behavior assessment** — emotional eating, binge eating, uncontrolled eating
- **Previous meditation experience** — naive vs experienced practitioners
- **Psychological readiness** — openness to experiential, body-focused work
- **Stress levels** — high-stress individuals benefit more
- **Depression screening** — mindfulness may initially increase distress in severe depression
- **Time availability** — daily 15-30 min practice commitment
- **Group preference** — most programs are group-based
- **Weight loss expectations** — align with modest outcomes
- **Comorbidity** — anxiety, chronic pain (mindfulness beneficial)

## Who It's For

- Emotional eaters / stress eaters
- Binge eating disorder (mild-moderate)
- Individuals who dislike restrictive diet rules
- People with high stress and mindless eating patterns
- Complementary to dietary protocols (not standalone for rapid loss)
- Contraindicated: severe depression (untreated), psychosis, active trauma (adapt practice)

## Cost & Availability

- Moderate: $200-500 for 8-week program
- MB-EAT programs available in major cities
- Apps: Headspace, Calm (eating-specific modules limited)
- Books: "Mindful Eating" (Jan Chozen Bays), "The Mindful Eating Workbook" (Vincci Tsui)
- Free resources: UCLA Mindful Awareness Research Center
