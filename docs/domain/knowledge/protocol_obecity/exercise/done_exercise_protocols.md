## Description

Exercise is a cornerstone of obesity management. Different modalities produce distinct benefits: aerobic exercise (AE) for weight/BMI reduction, high-intensity interval training (HIIT) for body composition and metabolic improvements, resistance training (RT) for muscle preservation. Combined training (CT = AE + RT) provides comprehensive benefits.

## Protocol Details

### Aerobic Exercise (AE) — Best for weight/BMI
- **Protocol**: 150-300 min/week moderate-intensity (40-60% HRR) or 75-150 min/week vigorous (60-85% HRR)
- **Typical**: Brisk walking, jogging, cycling, swimming 30-60 min × 5-7 days/week
- **Weight loss**: −2.35 kg (95% CI: −4.05 to −0.64); best for BW and BMI (S064)
- **Mechanism**: Direct caloric expenditure, improved lipid oxidation

### HIIT — Best for body composition & metabolism
- **Protocol**: 3-5 sessions/week, 4-10 × 1-4 min intervals at 85-95% HRmax, 1-3 min rest
- **Typical**: 20-30 min total (including warm-up/cool-down)
- **WC reduction**: −5.93 cm (95% CI: −10.71 to −1.15) — best among modalities (S064)
- **Body fat reduction**: −3.93% (best for %BF)
- **TG reduction**: −20.55 mg/dL; FBG: −14.31 mg/dL
- **VO2max improvement**: +7.41 mL/kg/min (best for CRF)
- **Superior to AE for**: waist circumference, %body fat, glucose, TG, VO2max (S064)

### Resistance Training (RT) — Best for muscle preservation
- **Protocol**: 2-3 sessions/week, 8-12 exercises, 8-15 reps at 60-80% 1RM
- **Metabolic improvement**: +3.16 VO2max, modest weight reduction
- **Critical for**: preserving lean mass during caloric restriction
- **Combined with AE/protein**: synergistic for body composition

## Pros

- AE: proven weight/BMI reduction, accessible, low injury risk
- HIIT: time-efficient, superior metabolic benefits, improves VO2max more than any other modality
- RT: preserves muscle mass during weight loss, prevents sarcopenic obesity
- CT: comprehensive benefits (body composition + cardiorespiratory fitness)
- Exercise alone produces modest weight loss (−2 to −6 kg) but significant metabolic benefits
- Combining exercise with dietary restriction improves outcomes vs diet alone
- No adverse effects when properly prescribed

## Cons

- Exercise alone: modest weight loss (AE −2.35 kg) — insufficient for clinically meaningful weight loss in severe obesity
- HIIT: higher injury risk, requires monitoring in deconditioned populations
- RT: equipment needed (gym membership or home weights)
- Adherence: 40-60% at 6 months for structured programs
- AE: requires significant time commitment (150-300 min/week)
- Weight loss from exercise plateaus as body adapts
- Exercise without caloric restriction produces minimal weight loss
- No modality significantly improves TC, HDL, LDL, or BP (S064)

## Personalization Data Needed

- **Current fitness level** — VO2max, 1RM testing or field tests
- **BMI and body composition** — AE for BMI ≥35; HIIT for metabolically unhealthy obesity
- **Age** — HIIT for 18-30; mixed for 31-40; MICT preferred >40 (S061)
- **Comorbidities** — CAD, joint disease (low-impact AE preferred); T2DM (HIIT beneficial)
- **Injury history** — musculoskeletal limitations
- **Time availability** — HIIT 20-30 min vs AE 30-60 min per session
- **Equipment access** — gym vs home vs outdoor
- **Exercise preference** — enjoyment predicts adherence
- **Weight loss goal** — modest with exercise alone; augment with diet/pharma for >5%
- **Supervision availability** — HIIT safest with initial supervision

## Who It's For

- All adults with overweight/obesity (foundation of obesity management)
- AE: primary for weight/BMI reduction; accessible for all fitness levels
- HIIT: metabolically unhealthy obesity, T2DM, time-constrained individuals
- RT: muscle preservation focus, sarcopenic obesity, older adults
- CT: comprehensive metabolic health improvement
- Contraindicated: uncontrolled CVD (evaluate before HIIT), severe orthopedic limitations (modify modality), uncontrolled hypertension (control BP before HIIT)

## Cost & Availability

- AE: free (walking/running outdoors); minimal cost (shoes)
- HIIT: free to low (bodyweight HIIT); gym membership $30-80/month
- RT: gym membership $30-80/month; home setup $200-500
- Classes: $10-30/session
- App/program: $10-30/month (Future, Peloton, Apple Fitness+)
- Worldwide: universally available (walking is free)
