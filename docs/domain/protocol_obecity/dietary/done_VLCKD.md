## Description

VLCKD combines ketogenic carbohydrate restriction (<50g/day) with severe caloric restriction (<800 kcal/day), typically using meal replacement products. Requires medical supervision. Induces rapid weight loss while preserving lean mass. Typically 4-12 week ketogenic phase followed by gradual refeeding.

## Protocol Details

- **Calories**: <800 kcal/day (usually 600–800 kcal)
- **Carbs**: <50 g/day (typically 30-50 g)
- **Protein**: 1.0–1.5 g/kg ideal body weight (muscle preservation)
- **Fat**: 15-30 g/day from natural sources
- **Duration**: Ketogenic phase 4-12 weeks → transition phase 4-8 weeks → maintenance
- **Format**: Ready-made meal replacements (shakes, bars, soups) + lean protein + vegetables
- **Adherence**: High during controlled phase (92.5% completion, S020); 7.5% discontinuation rate
- **Medical monitoring**: Monthly labs (electrolytes, kidney/liver function, lipids)

## Pros

- Most rapid weight loss among all dietary interventions
- Preserves lean mass (unique among calorie-restricted diets)
- Clinically meaningful weight loss (−15 kg in 4-12 weeks)
- High completion rate in medical setting
- Reverses metabolic syndrome components
- European guidelines recommend for obesity management (S022)

## Cons

- Must be medically supervised — not for DIY use
- High cost of meal replacement products
- Social isolation during ketogenic phase
- "Keto flu" (worse due to severe caloric deficit)
- Risk of electrolyte imbalances, dehydration
- Gallbladder risk (rapid weight loss → gallstones)
- Constipation (low fiber + low volume)
- Refeeding must be gradual to prevent weight regain
- Long-term efficacy dependent on transition phase success
- Not suitable for long-term use (>12-16 weeks)

## Personalization Data Needed

- **Full medical history + physical exam** — mandatory before starting
- **Age, sex, BMI, body composition (DEXA)** — baseline
- **Lipid panel, HbA1c, fasting glucose, insulin** — metabolic baseline
- **Electrolytes (Na, K, Mg, Ca)** — monitor during ketosis
- **Kidney function (creatinine, eGFR, uric acid)** — risk of stones
- **Liver function (ALT, AST, GGT)** — fatty liver improvement
- **ECG** — rule out arrhythmia risk
- **Medication review** — adjust diuretics, diabetes meds, antihypertensives
- **Gallbladder ultrasound** — screen for pre-existing gallstones
- **Psychological evaluation** — screen for ED, binge eating
- **Motivation & readiness** — high commitment required
- **Support system** — partner/family awareness

## Who It's For

- Class II-III obesity (BMI ≥35) requiring rapid pre-surgical weight loss
- Obesity with T2DM needing rapid glycemic improvement
- Obesity with metabolic syndrome requiring intensive intervention
- Individuals who failed less restrictive diets
- Contraindications: eating disorders, CKD (eGFR <45), liver failure, cardiac arrhythmias, porphyria, uncontrolled thyroid disease, pregnancy, breastfeeding, type 1 diabetes, severe psychiatric illness

## Cost & Availability

- High: $200–400/month for meal replacement products
- Medical supervision costs vary (telemedicine programs: $100–300/month)
- Commercial programs: Optifast, Medifast, LighterLife, Cambridge Diet
- Medical programs: hospital-based obesity clinics
- Not available OTC — requires physician prescription in most countries
