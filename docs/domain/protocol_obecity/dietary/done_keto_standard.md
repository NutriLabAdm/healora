## Description

A very low-carbohydrate, high-fat diet that induces metabolic ketosis (blood BHB >0.5 mmol/L). Standard macronutrient ratio: 70-80% fat, 15-25% protein, 5-10% carbs (<50g or <20-30g net carbs per day).

## Protocol Details

- **Carb limit**: <50 g/day total carbs (or <20-30 g net carbs)
- **Protein**: 1.2–2.0 g/kg body weight (moderate, not high)
- **Fat**: 70-80% of calories (saturated + unsaturated from whole foods)
- **Ketosis target**: Blood BHB 0.5–3.0 mmol/L; urine strips for tracking
- **Duration**: Induction 2-4 weeks → full keto 3-12 months → transition to low-carb maintenance
- **Adherence**: 50-70% at 6 months; social and practical barriers reduce long-term adherence

## Evidence

| Outcome | Effect | Certainty | Source |
|---------|--------|-----------|--------|
| Body weight loss (6 mo) | −3.7 to −9.1 kg vs control | High | S019, S021, S024 |
| Body weight loss (12 mo) | −5.6 to −6.3 kg (net carbs <10%) | Moderate | S023 |
| BMI reduction | −2.9 kg/m² | Moderate | S024 |
| Waist circumference | −7.6 cm | Moderate | S024 |
| Fat mass loss | −5.3 kg (preserves muscle vs LCD) | Moderate | S024, S019 |
| HbA1c reduction (T2DM) | −0.38 to −0.7% | High | S019, S024 |
| Triglycerides reduction | −30 mg/dL | High | S019 |
| LDL cholesterol | +6.4 mg/dL increase (12 mo) | High (increase) | S019 |
| HDL cholesterol increase | +0.28 SMD | Moderate | S024 |
| Muscle mass | −1.3 kg loss in healthy (not in VLCKD) | Moderate | S019 |

## Pros

- Most effective for rapid weight loss among all dietary patterns
- Reduced appetite and hunger (ketone bodies suppress ghrelin)
- Improved glycemic control and insulin sensitivity
- Triglycerides decrease significantly
- HDL increases
- Preserves muscle better than low-fat isocaloric diets (in VLCKD variant)
- Reduces inflammation and oxidative stress

## Cons

- High dropout rates (30-50% by 6 months)
- "Keto flu" in first 1-2 weeks: headache, fatigue, brain fog, constipation
- LDL cholesterol increases (clinically meaningful +6.4 mg/dL at 12 mo, S019)
- Very restrictive — social eating becomes difficult
- Kidney stone risk (increased uric acid excretion)
- Long-term safety data limited beyond 2 years
- May cause micronutrient deficiencies (fiber, vitamin C, magnesium)
- Not recommended with某些 medications (SGLT2 inhibitors risk of euglycemic DKA)

## Personalization Data Needed

- **Age, sex, BMI, body composition (DEXA)** — baseline
- **Lipid panel (TC, LDL, HDL, TG)** — monitor LDL increase
- **Fasting glucose, HbA1c, fasting insulin** — insulin resistance status
- **Kidney function (creatinine, eGFR)** — risk of stones with high protein
- **Blood pressure** — may drop significantly
- **Medication review** — diabetes meds (risk of hypoglycemia), diuretics, antihypertensives
- **Gastrointestinal health** — constipation management
- **Exercise type & volume** — performance may drop during adaptation
- **Food preferences & cultural eating patterns** — keto incompatible with many cuisines
- **Psychological readiness** — screen for ED, orthorexia, strict all-or-nothing tendency
- **Previous dieting history** — yo-yo dieters at risk for keto-binge cycles

## Who It's For

- Adults with obesity needing ≥10 kg weight loss
- Type 2 diabetes / prediabetes (glycemic control)
- Metabolic syndrome
- PCOS
- Individuals who prefer fat/satiety over carb restriction
- Not recommended: eating disorder history, CKD (eGFR <30), pancreatitis, gallbladder disease (without monitoring), pregnancy, breastfeeding, type 1 diabetes (except with expert medical management)

## Cost & Availability

- Moderate-high cost ($100–150/week) — high-quality fats (avocado, olive oil, grass-fed meat) are expensive
- Testing supplies: ketone strips/blood meter ($20–50/month)
- Low-cost version possible (eggs, canned fish, chicken thighs)
- App support: Carb Manager, Cronometer, KetoDiet
- Books: "The Art and Science of Low Carbohydrate Living" (Volek & Phinney)
