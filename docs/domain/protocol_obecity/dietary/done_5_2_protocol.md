## Description

The 5:2 diet involves 5 days of normal (ad libitum) eating and 2 non-consecutive days of severe energy restriction (~500–600 kcal for women/men). Also called Whole Day Fasting (WDF) or Intermittent Energy Restriction (IER).

## Protocol Details

- **Regimen**: 5 days ad libitum + 2 non-consecutive fast days (e.g., Monday & Thursday)
- **Fast day intake**: ~500 kcal (women), ~600 kcal (men), typically 1-2 small meals
- **Duration**: RCTs 8–52 weeks
- **Adherence**: Moderate (60-75%) — easier than ADF, harder than TRE
- **Variation**: 4:3 diet (4 feed days, 3 fast days) studied in some trials

## Evidence

| Outcome | Effect | Certainty | Source |
|---------|--------|-----------|--------|
| Body weight loss | −2.5 to −4.0 kg vs ad-libitum | Moderate | S001, S004 |
| Comparison to CER | Similar weight loss (no significant difference) | Moderate | S004 |
| Total cholesterol | Significant reduction vs TRE | Moderate | S001 |
| Fat mass reduction | −0.7 to −1.5 kg | Low | S003 |

## Pros

- Flexible: choose fasting days based on schedule
- Less socially disruptive than ADF
- Only 2 days per week require significant willpower
- Well-suited for people who prefer clear "on/off" days

## Cons

- Significant hunger on fast days
- Risk of overeating on feed days (compensation)
- Complex tracking on fast days (must count calories on those days)
- Headaches, fatigue, irritability on fast days
- Lower evidence quality than ADF or TRE

## Who It's For

- Adults with overweight/obesity who want flexibility
- People who cannot commit to daily restriction
- Not recommended for: underweight, ED history, insulin-dependent diabetes, pregnancy, breastfeeding

## Personalization Data Needed

- **Age, sex, BMI, body composition** — baseline
- **TDEE** — to calculate fast-day calorie target (~500–600 kcal)
- **Schedule flexibility** — choose 2 non-consecutive fast days (Mon+Thu vs Tue+Sat)
- **Social calendar** — avoid fast days on social eating occasions
- **Food preferences on fast days** — high-volume low-calorie foods preferred
- **Emotional eating triggers** — fast days may trigger binge on feed days
- **Previous dieting history & outcomes** — predict adherence pattern
- **Support system** — partner/family awareness and support

## Cost & Availability

- Free. Promoted by Michael Mosley (UK), popularized via books and media.
- App support: Zero, FastHabit, Simple
